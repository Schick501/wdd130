The wonderful world of Church communication is a dynamic, fast-paced part of the global Church. Serving on a Church communication council can be very rewarding. Through these efforts council members play a vital role in efforts to bring the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints out of obscurity.

Stake communication work is supervised by a member of the stake presidency, who helps establish local communication goals. Including relationships with local key opinion leaders, service in the community, humanitarian donations, and engaging in interfaith conversations and messaging to an external and sometimes internal audience. Local Coordinating Council Communication Councils provide expertise, support, and training.

Communication councils have two main areas of focus: outreach to opinion leaders and communicating positive messages. Collaborating as a communication council is important. Communication Councils deliver key messages externally to opinion leader and communities, and internally to Church members.

Council volunteers are trained in the importance of establishing connections and building mutually beneficial key relationships. Building these relationships is not missionary work. Relationships with those not of our faith are invaluable to the Church, prompting changes in attitude and misprections about the Church, its doctrine, and its members. Priesthood leaders guide these strategic communications.
